catch the moonlight in your fingers

let the tendrils sweep through your hair

kiss the stars that litter the sky

you're already somewhere

she watches the sky

dip below blue

from behind the glass window.

fingers extended,

they lightly trace

the horizon's silhouette,

a burst of orange on her tongue.

arms longing

arms falling

the warmth recedes into the night

pulling a piece of her with it.

she picks one out

from the bowl,

its scent still fresh.

her own little sunset,

skin on flesh.
